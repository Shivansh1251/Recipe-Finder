Dal Makhni

Dal Makhani is a popular North Indian dish made with black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), cooked with various spices and finished with cream and butter. It's a rich and flavorful dish that is often enjoyed with rice or Indian bread like naan or roti. Here's a basic recipe for making Dal Makhani:

Ingredients:

- 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal) - 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma) - 1 large onion, finely chopped - 2 tomatoes, pureed - 3 cloves garlic, minced - 1-inch piece of ginger, grated - 1 green chili, chopped (adjust according to spice preference) - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - 1 teaspoon garam masala - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - Salt to taste - 2 tablespoons butter - 2 tablespoons cream - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1. Soaking the Lentils and Beans: - Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly. - Soak them in water overnight or for at least 6-8 hours. 2. Cooking Lentils and Beans: - After soaking, drain the water and rinse the lentils and beans. - In a pressure cooker, add the lentils, kidney beans, turmeric powder, and salt. - Add enough water to cover the lentils and beans. - Pressure cook until the lentils and beans are soft and well-cooked. 3. Making the Dal Makhani: - In a large pan, heat butter. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. - Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. - Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chili. Sauté for a minute. - Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala. 4. Adding Spices: - Add red chili powder and garam masala. Cook for a couple of minutes. - Mash some of the cooked lentils and beans and add them to the pan. Mix well. 5. Combining Everything: - Add the remaining cooked lentils and beans along with any water they were cooked in. - Mix well and let it simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. 6. Finishing Touches: - Add cream and mix well. Simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes. - Adjust salt and spice levels according to your taste. 7. Garnish and Serve: - Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. - Serve hot with rice or Indian bread like naan or roti. Enjoy your homemade Dal Makhani!

Shahi Paneer

Shahi Paneer is a rich and creamy Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) in a flavorful and aromatic cashew and tomato-based gravy. It's a popular vegetarian dish that's often served on special occasions or as part of a festive meal. Here's a simple recipe for Shahi Paneer:

Ingredients:

For the Paneer Marinade:
- 250 grams paneer, cut into cubes - 1/4 cup thick yogurt - 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste - 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder - Salt to taste
For the Shahi Gravy:
- 1 cup cashews, soaked in warm water for 2 hours - 2 large onions, finely chopped - 2 tomatoes, chopped - 1/4 cup fresh cream - 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon garam masala - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder - 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder - Salt to taste - 1 tablespoon honey or sugar (optional, for sweetness) - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Paneer Marinade: 1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, and salt. 2. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them well. 3. Allow the paneer to marinate for at least 30 minutes. Shahi Gravy: 1. Grind the soaked cashews into a smooth paste using a little water. 2. Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter. 3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. 4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate. 5. Add the cashew paste and cook for a few minutes until the mixture thickens. 6. Add garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and cardamom powder. Mix well. 7. Add fresh cream and stir to combine. Adjust the consistency with water if needed. 8. Season with salt and add honey or sugar if you prefer a slightly sweet taste. 9. Allow the gravy to simmer on low heat for 5-7 minutes. Putting It Together: 1. In a separate pan, grill or shallow fry the marinated paneer until golden brown. 2. Add the paneer to the simmering Shahi gravy. 3. Mix gently, ensuring the paneer is coated with the rich gravy. 4. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. 5. Serve Shahi Paneer hot with naan, roti, or rice. Enjoy the indulgent and flavorful Shahi Paneer!

Manchurian

Vegetable Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that consists of deep-fried vegetable dumplings served in a flavorful and tangy sauce. There are two main components to Vegetable Manchurian: the vegetable dumplings and the Manchurian sauce. Here's a basic recipe for making Vegetable Manchurian:

Ingredients:

For Vegetable Dumplings:
- 1 cup finely chopped mixed vegetables (cabbage, carrot, bell pepper, beans) - 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida) - 1/4 cup cornflour - 1 tablespoon soy sauce - 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste - Salt to taste - Oil for deep frying
For Manchurian Sauce:
- 1 tablespoon oil - 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic - 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger - 1/2 cup finely chopped onions - 1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers - 2 tablespoons soy sauce - 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup - 1 tablespoon red chili sauce (adjust to taste) - 1 tablespoon vinegar - 1 tablespoon cornflour mixed with 1/4 cup water (for thickening) - 1 teaspoon sugar - Salt to taste - 2 cups water

Instructions:

Vegetable Dumplings:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine chopped vegetables, all-purpose flour, cornflour, soy sauce, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. 2. Mix well to form a dough. If the mixture is too dry, you can add a little water. 3. Shape the mixture into small, round balls or dumplings. 4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the vegetable dumplings until golden brown and crisp. Remove and set aside on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
Manchurian Sauce:
1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. 2. Add chopped garlic and ginger. Sauté for a minute until they become fragrant. 3. Add chopped onions and bell peppers. Cook until they become soft and slightly caramelized. 4. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, vinegar, sugar, and salt. 5. Pour the sauce mixture into the pan and stir well. 6. In a separate bowl, mix cornflour with water to make a smooth slurry. 7. Add the cornflour slurry to the pan and mix continuously to avoid lumps. 8. Add 2 cups of water and continue stirring until the sauce thickens. 9. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.
Combining Dumplings and Sauce:
1. Just before serving, add the fried vegetable dumplings to the Manchurian sauce. 2. Gently coat the dumplings with the sauce, making sure they are well coated. 3. Garnish with chopped spring onions or cilantro. Serve Vegetable Manchurian hot with fried rice or noodles. Enjoy your homemade Indo-Chinese delight!

Rajma

Rajma, also known as kidney beans, is a popular legume in Indian cuisine. One of the most loved dishes made with rajma is Rajma Masala, a flavorful and hearty curry served with rice or Indian bread. Here's a basic recipe for making Rajma Masala:

Ingredients:

- 1 cup dry rajma (kidney beans) - 2 large onions, finely chopped - 2 large tomatoes, pureed - 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste - 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust according to spice preference) - 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro), for garnish - 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter) - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - 1 tablespoon coriander powder - 1 teaspoon garam masala - Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. Preparation of Rajma: - Wash the rajma thoroughly and soak them in plenty of water overnight or for at least 8 hours. - Drain the water, rinse the rajma, and cook them in a pressure cooker with enough water until they are soft and well-cooked. 2. Making Rajma Masala: - Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. - Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown. - Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears. - Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala. - Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. 3. Combining Rajma and Masala: - Add the cooked rajma to the masala and mix well. - Add salt and water if needed to achieve the desired consistency. - Simmer the curry on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. 4. Garnish and Serve: - Garnish the Rajma Masala with chopped coriander leaves. - Serve hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, or Indian bread like naan or roti. Rajma Masala is a wholesome and delicious dish that's enjoyed by many. Adjust the spice levels according to your preference and enjoy this classic Indian comfort food.

Chana Masala

Chana Masala, also known as Chole Masala, is a popular North Indian dish made with chickpeas (chana) cooked in a flavorful and aromatic tomato-based gravy. Here's a basic recipe for making Chana Masala:

Ingredients:

For Boiling Chickpeas:
- 1 cup dried chickpeas (chana) - Water for soaking and boiling - 1/2 teaspoon baking soda (optional, for boiling)
For Chana Masala:
- 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter) - 1 large onion, finely chopped - 2 large tomatoes, pureed - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste - 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust according to spice preference) - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon coriander powder - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - 1 teaspoon garam masala - 1 teaspoon cumin powder - 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur) - Salt to taste - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Boiling Chickpeas:
1. Wash the dried chickpeas thoroughly and soak them in water overnight or for at least 8 hours. 2. Drain the water and rinse the chickpeas. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked chickpeas, water, and baking soda (if using). 3. Pressure cook until the chickpeas are soft and well-cooked. If you don't have a pressure cooker, you can boil them in a regular pot, but it will take longer.
Chana Masala:
1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. 2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown. 3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears. 4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala. 5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and dry mango powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. 6. Add the boiled chickpeas along with some of the water they were cooked in. Adjust the consistency according to your preference. 7. Season with salt and let the Chana Masala simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
Garnish and Serve:
1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. 2. Serve hot with rice, naan, roti, or any Indian bread of your choice. Chana Masala is a hearty and flavorful dish that's enjoyed across the Indian subcontinent. Adjust the spice levels according to your taste, and enjoy this delicious and nutritious chickpea curry!

Raita

Raita is a popular Indian side dish made with yogurt, and it often includes various ingredients like vegetables, fruits, or spices. It serves as a cooling and refreshing accompaniment to spicy Indian meals. Here's a basic recipe for making a simple Cucumber Raita:

Ingredients:

- 1 cup plain yogurt (curd) - 1/2 cup cucumber, finely grated or chopped - 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder - 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin seeds (crushed) - 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala - Salt to taste - 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional) - Mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Yogurt: - In a bowl, whisk the plain yogurt until smooth. 2. Add Cucumber: - Add finely grated or chopped cucumber to the yogurt. If using grated cucumber, you can squeeze out excess water before adding it. 3. Seasoning: - Add cumin powder, crushed roasted cumin seeds, chaat masala, and salt to the yogurt. Mix well to combine. 4. Garnish: - Garnish the raita with chopped fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves if desired. 5. Chill and Serve: - Refrigerate the raita for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld and to serve it chilled. 6. Serve: - Serve the cucumber raita as a side dish with biryani, pulao, kebabs, or any spicy Indian meal.

Variations:

- Boondi Raita: Add a handful of boondi (crispy chickpea flour balls) to the yogurt for a crunchy texture. - Tomato Onion Raita: Add finely chopped tomatoes and onions to the yogurt for a tangy and crunchy variation. - Mint Raita: Add finely chopped mint leaves or mint powder for a refreshing flavor. Raita is a versatile dish, and you can customize it according to your taste preferences. It not only adds a burst of flavor but also helps to balance the heat in spicy dishes.

Butter Naan

Butter Naan is a popular Indian bread that is soft, fluffy, and typically brushed with butter. It's a perfect accompaniment to various Indian dishes, especially curries. Here's a simple recipe to make Butter Naan at home:

Ingredients:

- 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida) - 1 teaspoon sugar - 1 teaspoon baking powder - 1/4 teaspoon baking soda - 1/4 teaspoon salt - 2 tablespoons plain yogurt - 2 tablespoons melted ghee or oil - Warm water (as needed to make a soft dough) - Butter for brushing

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough: - In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. - Add yogurt and melted ghee or oil. Mix well. - Gradually add warm water and knead the ingredients into a soft and smooth dough. - Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 2-3 hours, allowing it to rise. 2. Divide and Shape: - After the dough has rested, divide it into equal-sized portions (usually golf ball-sized). - Take one portion and roll it into a ball. Roll the ball into a flat, oval or round shape, about 1/4 inch thick. 3. Cooking Naan: - Preheat a tawa (flat pan) or a griddle on medium-high heat. - Place the rolled naan on the hot surface. Cook until bubbles start to form on the surface. - Flip the naan and cook the other side until golden brown spots appear. 4. Finish with Butter: - Once the naan is cooked, remove it from the pan and brush it with melted butter. 5. Repeat: - Repeat the process for the remaining portions of dough. 6. Serve: - Serve Butter Naan hot with your favorite curry, dal, or any Indian dish.

Tips:

- You can also bake the naan in a preheated oven at 475°F (245°C) until puffed and golden brown. - To enhance the flavor, you can add minced garlic or chopped cilantro to the dough before rolling it out. - If you don't have a tawa or griddle, you can use a skillet or even a barbecue grill to cook the naan. Enjoy your homemade Butter Naan with your favorite Indian dishes!

Missi Roti

Missi Roti is a traditional Indian flatbread made from a combination of whole wheat flour (atta) and gram flour (besan). It's often flavored with various spices and herbs, making it a flavorful and nutritious option. Here's a simple recipe to make Missi Roti:

Ingredients:

- 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta) - 1/2 cup gram flour (besan) - 1/2 cup finely chopped onions - 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander leaves - 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste) - 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste - 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - Salt to taste - Water (as needed to make the dough) - Ghee or oil for cooking

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough: - In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, gram flour, chopped onions, chopped coriander leaves, green chili, ginger-garlic paste, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. - Gradually add water and knead the ingredients into a soft and pliable dough. 2. Divide and Shape: - Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. - Roll each ball into a flat, round roti using a rolling pin. The thickness can be similar to that of a regular chapati. 3. Cooking Missi Roti: - Heat a tawa (flat pan) or griddle on medium heat. - Place the rolled Missi Roti on the hot surface. - Cook until bubbles start to appear on the surface, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown spots appear. 4. Finish with Ghee or Oil: - Brush the cooked Missi Roti with ghee or oil on both sides. 5. Repeat: - Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls. 6. Serve: - Serve Missi Roti hot with yogurt, pickle, or any Indian curry.

Tips:

- You can customize the recipe by adding chopped spinach, fenugreek leaves (methi), or other herbs for added flavor and nutrition. - Adjust the spice levels according to your preference. Missi Roti is a wholesome and flavorful flatbread that pairs well with a variety of Indian dishes. Enjoy it as a part of your meal for a nutritious and delicious experience!

Jeera Rice

Jeera rice, also known as cumin rice, is a simple and flavorful Indian rice dish made with basmati rice and cumin seeds. It's a versatile accompaniment that pairs well with various Indian curries and gravies. Here's a basic recipe for making Jeera Rice:

Ingredients:

- 1 cup basmati rice - 2 cups water - 2 tablespoons ghee or oil - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 2-3 green cardamom pods - 2-3 cloves - 1-inch cinnamon stick - Salt to taste - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Rinse the Rice: - Rinse the basmati rice under cold running water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming sticky. 2. Soak the Rice: - Soak the rinsed rice in water for about 20-30 minutes. This helps the rice cook evenly. 3. Drain the Rice: - After soaking, drain the water from the rice using a fine-mesh sieve. 4. Cooking Jeera Rice: - In a pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. - Add cumin seeds, cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for a minute until the cumin seeds splutter and release their aroma. 5. Add Rice: - Add the drained basmati rice to the pan. Stir and sauté for 2-3 minutes to coat the rice with the cumin-infused ghee. 6. Add Water: - Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt to taste. Bring it to a boil. 7. Simmer: - Once it boils, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed. 8. Fluff the Rice: - Once cooked, fluff the rice gently with a fork to separate the grains. 9. Garnish: - Optionally, garnish with fresh coriander leaves. 10. Serve: - Serve Jeera Rice hot with your favorite curry, dal, or any Indian dish. Jeera Rice is a simple and aromatic side dish that adds a fragrant touch to your meal. It's a staple in Indian cuisine and is easy to make at home.

Curry

Vegetable curry is a versatile dish that can be made in various styles using a mix of vegetables and aromatic spices. Here's a basic recipe for a simple and delicious vegetable curry:

Ingredients:

- 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, potatoes, peas, beans, cauliflower, etc.), chopped - 1 large onion, finely chopped - 2 tomatoes, chopped - 1/2 cup tomato puree (optional) - 2 tablespoons oil or ghee - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - 1 tablespoon coriander powder - 1 teaspoon garam masala - Salt to taste - 1/2 cup plain yogurt (optional) - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Vegetables: - Wash and chop the vegetables into bite-sized pieces. 2. Sauté Aromatics: - Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. - Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. 3. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: - Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears. 4. Add Tomatoes: - Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate. 5. Spices: - Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. 6. Add Vegetables: - Add the chopped vegetables and mix well to coat them with the spice mixture. 7. Cooking Vegetables: - Cover the pan and let the vegetables cook on medium heat until they are tender. You may need to add a little water to prevent the mixture from sticking to the pan. 8. Optional Yogurt: - If using yogurt, you can add it at this stage. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes. 9. Adjust Seasoning: - Adjust salt and spice levels according to your taste. 10. Garnish and Serve: - Garnish the vegetable curry with fresh coriander leaves. - Serve hot with rice, naan, or any Indian bread of your choice. Feel free to customize this basic vegetable curry by adding your favorite vegetables or adjusting the spices to suit your taste. It's a flexible recipe that you can make according to what you have on hand. Enjoy your homemade vegetable curry!
